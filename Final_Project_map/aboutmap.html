Map Information

This Web Map serves as an interactive guide to parks and hiking / biking trails in La Crosse Wisconsin and the surrounding counties.
I wanted to make this map because I have personally found that finding information on new places to hike can be daunting, and require searching through multiple websites. I believe this map can consolidate otherwise scattered information for avid enjoyers of the outdoors, or those in the La Crosse area who seek to get more into outdoor activity, but do not know where to look

Methodology

Spatial data was acquired from the Wisconsin DNR GIS Open Data Portal, the Minnesota Geospatial Commons, and La Crosse County GIS resources. Trails in Great River Bluffs State Park, Perot State Park, Goose Island County Park, and Reno Recration area were manually digitized with the help of AllTrails due to a lack of opensource data on trails in those parks. All datasets were projected to WGS 1984, exported as GeoJSON, and added to the map using fetch. Amenity scores were assigned manually on a scale of 1–5 based on available facility information. Trail difficulty classifications were sourced from existing attribute data from the La Crosse County Trails Dataset.

Data Credits

WI State Park Polygon Boundaries: Wisconsin DNR GIS Open Data Portal (data-wi-dnr.opendata.arcgis.com)
MN State Park Polygon Boundaries: Minnesota DNR via Minnesota Geospatial Commons (gisdata.mn.gov)
park boundaries and trails: La Crosse County Land Information GIS Office
Basemap tiles: OpenStreetMap, CARTO
Mapping library: Leaflet.js (leafletjs.com).

Project Notes

I believe that the simplicity of this project is a big strength. I originally wanted to add several more factors such as boat launches, campsites, and beaches, but time contraints and difficulty finding open source data led me to deciding on 2 thematic layers and 2 geometry layers. However, I think this makes the map very concise and to the point. The 2 thematic layers that I currently have work well with each other, and I didn't experience any conflicts or difficulty with layers loading. Finding data for trails in lesser known state parks was much more difficult than I expected, and I ran into data loss while I was digitizing trail data for the last several parks. Before the final submission, I plan to re-digitize the trails that I lost, and add a point layer for trailheads with a popup.
